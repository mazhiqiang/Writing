The conventional SMC design approach consists of two steps. First, a sliding manifold is designed such that the system trajectory along the manifold acquires certain desired properties. Then, a discontinuous control is designed such that the system trajectories reach the manifold in finite time\cite{Zhu2011Adaptive}.\\
Attitude kinematics represented by four unitary quaternions is given as follows:\\
\begin{align}
\begin{bmatrix}
\dot{q}_0\\\dot{q}
\end{bmatrix}
=1/2Q^T(q_0,q)\omega
\end{align}
where $q_0=cos(\Phi/2)$ and $q=[q_1\quad q_2\quad q_3]^T=\Gamma sin(\Phi/2)$ are subject to the constraint $q_0^2+q^Tq=1$.Here, $\Phi$ denotes the rotation angle about the Euler axis, which is determined by the unitary vector $\Gamma$, $Q$ with $q$ satisfying\\
\begin{align}
q = \begin{bmatrix}
0 &-q_3 &q_2\\q_3 &0 &-q_1\\-q_2 &q_1 &0
\end{bmatrix}
\end{align}
and $\omega$ the angular velocity of the unformed spacecraft in the body fixed frame.\cite{Hu2009Robust}
